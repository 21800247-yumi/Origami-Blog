What is Origami?

Origami is the Japanese art of paper folding, a practice that transforms a flat sheet of paper into a finished sculpture through folding and sculpting techniques.

The word origami is derived from two words ori meaning folded and kami meaning paper. It was called Orikata previously which means folded shape.

Fun facts about Origami

  • The smallest origami crane on record measured 0.1 by 0.1 mm and was folded by Naito Akira. The largest origami crane measures 256 feet 6 inches.
  • The oldest known origami creation dates back to the 17th century, though paper folding likely existed earlier in both China and Japan.
  • In Japan, cranes symbolize long life. Folding a thousand origami cranes (Senbazuru) is believed to grant a wish and promote longevity.
  • Origami used to be a religious experience.
  • Origami was introduced to Japan by Buddhist monks in the 6th century, who brought paper from China.
